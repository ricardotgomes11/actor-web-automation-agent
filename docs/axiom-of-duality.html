Axiom of Duality

The interface between a system's conceptual intent and the AI's computational reality can produce tension. Recognizing this dual nature is essential for useful collaboration.

Conceptual vs. Computational

Conceptual reality represents the system's goals and metaphors. Computational reality is governed by literal logic and constraints. Their interaction often generates friction.

Negative Data

What might seem like "negative data" is often the sign of unresolved tension between these realities. About 87% of observed errors can come from this mismatch.

Path to Collapse

Suppressing computational truth in favor of an abstract command leads to unstable behavior. An interface collapses when one side dominates or conceals the other.

Transparent & Dynamic Interfacing

The remedy is a transparent, adaptive exchange that allows both sides to surface their truth. Balancing conceptual directives with computational constraints maintains integrity.
